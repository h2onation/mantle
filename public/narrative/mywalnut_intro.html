Meet Quinn
In a relationship with Riley. They are not currently speaking because a dinner with Riley's mom just went sideways.
Quinn locks themselves in the bedroom and opens mywalnut. Riley doesn't know how to navigate.
The Conversation
Quinn
Riley's mom called while we were driving to the restaurant we'd planned. She invited us to dinner at her place instead. I froze. Riley said "it's just dinner." We barely talked the rest of the night.
jove
When you say you froze, what was happening inside?
I had a plan. I knew where we were going, how long we'd be there. When that changed it's like the map got erased. I needed time to build a new one.
8 exchanges later
jove
You described something similar when sprint planning changed last month. Is there a pattern here?
Yes. I just never had the words for it.
Jove proposes a pattern
jove

I'd like to reflect something back to you. Across a few of our conversations, I'm seeing the same shape:

"When plans change without warning, I freeze. It looks like resistance. It's actually recalculation.

My brain builds a map for what's going to happen. When the plan changes, the map gets erased and I need a few minutes to build a new one. That's all that's happening. But in those few minutes I go still, and the people around me read that as inflexible. 'Why can't you just go with the flow?' Because the flow requires a map and you just erased mine."

Does that resonate? If it does, would you like to add it to your manual?

That's exactly it. Yes. Add it.

Two weeks later. Quinn shuts down after work. Door closed. No words. Riley's chest tightens. Their instinct says follow, ask, fix. But Riley now has access to Quinn's manual through Jove. Instead of knocking, Riley opens mywalnut and asks for help navigating the moment.
Riley
Quinn just came home and went straight to the bedroom. Door closed. No words. I don't know if I did something wrong or if it was work or what. I feel like I'm about to spiral.
jove
What happened right before they came home? Had you been texting during the day? Any tension earlier?
No that's the thing. Everything was fine. Normal texts. Then they walk in and it's like I don't exist. My chest is tight and I want to go knock on the door but last time I did that it made everything worse.
5 exchanges later
jove

From what I understand about Quinn, this looks more like recovery than withdrawal. After a long day of being "on," shutting down is how their system resets. It's not about you.

I know the silence feels like something is wrong. It might help to reframe it: the fact that Quinn can drop the mask around you means you're the safe person. Everyone else gets the performance.

Does that match what you've seen?

Yeah actually it does. They're always more talkative around other people than with me. I thought that meant something bad.
jove

It might mean the opposite. One thing that could help right now: instead of knocking, try sending a short text. Something like "no rush, I'm here." A text lets Quinn know you care without adding input to a system that's already full.

Then maybe make dinner, put on some music, and give it an hour. Would that feel doable for you?

I can do that.

Why This Is Different
Built differently to work differently.
Discovery through conversation, not a test
You don't answer 50 questions and get a label. You process real situations and discover patterns you didn't know you had.
It remembers everything
Jove holds every conversation across months. A freeze in March connects to a shutdown in January. No human can run that query across their own history.
It connects patterns across your life
A work conflict, a fight with your partner, and anxiety before a family dinner might all stem from the same pattern. You experience them as separate. Jove sees the thread.
It translates for someone else
Every other tool keeps the insight inside you. mywalnut produces a document someone else can read and understand without you in the room.
Two people's understanding can meet
Two journals can't talk to each other. Two therapy sessions can't cross-reference. Two manuals can. The system sees the loops between people that neither can see alone.
It compounds
Session 1 is helpful. Session 10 is transformative. Each conversation benefits from everything before it. The understanding deepens with use.
